Atkore makes electrical conduit and related "must-stock" building products, sold mainly through distributors to contractors. Its profits swing with the price gap between what it charges and what its raw materials (steel, copper, PVC) cost — and that gap is near a cyclical low. Adjusted EBITDA of $321m over the last twelve months is under a quarter of the 2022 peak because pandemic-era conduit prices unwound; volumes, though, held up. The base case assumes a gradual margin recovery (to about 15% by fiscal 2030), and at today's $72.43 the shares already price in most of that recovery.

What it is

Atkore runs two business segments, both evaluated by management on net sales and Adjusted EBITDA and sold mainly through electrical and industrial distributors to contractors:

Electrical (~70% of sales)

Conduit, cable and fittings that carry and protect building wiring — the high-margin but highly cyclical core. Its EBITDA margin swung from 42% in FY2022 to 16.5% in FY2025 as conduit pricing normalized.

Safety & Infrastructure (~30% of sales)

Metal framing, mechanical tube, cable management and perimeter security for construction and industrial markets — structurally lower-margin (12.8% in FY2025) but far steadier, the ballast when Electrical fell.

Valuation

The discounted-cash-flow model puts fair value at about $62 per share (range $55–74), a little below the $72.43 price — so the shares are roughly fairly valued, with the market already paying for the assumed recovery. On peer multiples ATKR looks cheap (EV/EBITDA 8.6x vs about 16x for core peers, forward P/E about 12x vs 20x) and offers a ~5.9% free-cash-flow yield, but that discount is the market's compensation for its greater exposure to commodity-price cycles and for earnings sitting at a cyclical low.

What's already priced in

  • The price already assumes the margin recovery to about 15% by FY2030 largely plays out — the reverse-engineered DCF implies only ~3.5% long-run growth, in line with the base case rather than a stretch.
  • The deep discount to peers on EV/EBITDA and P/E is real, but it is the market's compensation for ATKR's commodity-price cyclicality and for its earnings being at a cyclical trough.
  • Upside from here needs margins to exceed 15%, or the valuation multiple to re-rate toward the historical ~8x exit; downside opens if margins stall near the prior ~13% path (worth roughly −$13 per share).

Main risks

  • Pricing power is conditional — new PVC/steel capacity and lean distributor inventories could cap the price recovery unless tariffs hold; a plateau near ~13% margins is worth about −$13 per share.
  • Customer concentration — Sonepar is over 10% of sales and the top ten customers are ~40%; volume held in the downturn partly because Atkore conceded price.
  • Litigation and governance overhangs — a $136.5m PVC antitrust settlement with a securities case still open, plus a CEO search underway and a CFO under two years in the seat.

Bottom line

Fair Value — wait. At $72.43 the shares already reflect the mid-cycle recovery the base case assumes, so risk and reward are balanced. We would look to buy on a pullback into the $65–68 zone (still inside the DCF range) or on a confirmed technical breakout, rather than at today's level.
