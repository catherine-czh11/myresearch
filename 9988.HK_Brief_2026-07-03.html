The value case is unambiguous, but the share price is still falling — stage the entry rather than committing all at once.

Alibaba is priced as a business in structural decline at the exact moment its story is turning. The fiscal year ended March 2026 carried a deliberate profit trough — management chose to spend heavily on rapid-delivery shopping ("quick commerce") and on China's largest AI and cloud build-out, cutting Adjusted EBITDA by 44% and pushing free cash flow negative. The market has treated that temporary spending as permanent: the shares trade at the bottom of their 52-week range while the core e-commerce franchise still earns a ~19% segment margin and the cloud business grows over 30% a year. Value should surface as delivery subsidies wind down, cloud and AI keep compounding, and share buybacks resume once cash flow turns positive.

What it is

Alibaba runs China's largest online marketplaces and its #1 cloud-computing platform. Merchants pay for advertising and commissions; consumers shop on Taobao, Tmall and the new instant-delivery service; and businesses rent computing power and AI tools. Four reporting segments:

China E-commerce (54% of revenue)

Taobao/Tmall advertising and commissions, direct sales, quick commerce and wholesale — the profit engine, earning a 19.4% margin even while funding the delivery war.

Cloud Intelligence (15% of revenue)

China's #1 cloud, growing 34% with AI products growing triple-digit for 11 straight quarters; a 9.0% margin that management expects to improve.

International commerce (14% of revenue)

AliExpress, Lazada and Trendyol — losses narrowed from RMB15.1bn to RMB2.1bn and the unit is approaching breakeven.

All others (25% of revenue)

Logistics, grocery, mapping, health and the Qwen consumer AI app — this is where the AI land-grab spending sits (a RMB35.7bn loss).

Valuation

The discounted-cash-flow model values the shares at HK$224.63 in the base case, with a bear-to-bull range of HK$116.21–285.43 — the entire range sits above the HK$94.10 price. On multiples the stock trades at 13.5x EV/EBITDA against a core-peer median of 12.6x (on depressed, war-trough earnings for everyone) and at 10.2x forward earnings versus 12.0x for the peer group. Free cash flow yield is negative (−3.3%) this year because of the investment peak — the model has it recovering above the cost of capital by FY2029. The market price implies either a ~16% discount rate or a business shrinking ~10% a year forever — assumptions the report finds extreme.

Why it's cheap — and why that should change

  • The market is capitalizing a self-inflicted trough: roughly RMB86bn of this year's profit decline was discretionary subsidy and AI spending, not lost franchise earnings — as delivery losses normalize from FY2028, reported profits rebuild.
  • The RMB974bn portfolio of investments (including ~33% of Ant Group) is being valued at close to zero; the balance sheet alone covers a large share of the market value.
  • Cloud and AI are compounding at 30%+ with pricing power from industry-wide hardware scarcity — a second profit engine the direct e-commerce peers do not own, and the clearest signal that would re-rate the stock.

Main risks

  • The delivery subsidy war lasts longer than expected — if losses persist past FY2028, the profit-recovery path that carries most of the valuation fails.
  • AI overbuild — management plans to spend far beyond its original RMB380bn three-year budget; if AI revenue disappoints, heavy depreciation permanently lowers margins.
  • Regulation and geopolitics — Chinese platform rules, US chip export controls and the 2021 antitrust fine show how quickly the environment can reprice the stock.

Bottom line

Attractive — but stage the entry. The shares are worth materially more than today's price on every method tested, yet the stock is in a confirmed downtrend (24.9% below its falling 100-day average). Defer full commitment until the price reclaims roughly HK$125 with improving momentum, or the HK$88.65 52-week low proves itself as a floor; the fundamental green light is the first quarter showing smaller quick-commerce losses alongside cloud growth of 35% or better.
