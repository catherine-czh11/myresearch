ISDN Holdings is an industrial-automation distributor and engineer serving China and Southeast Asia. Last year's headline revenue growth of +18.2% was driven almost entirely by construction revenue from a renewable-energy project, not by a real improvement in the core business. The core motion-control and specialized-engineering businesses are growing steadily on genuine, long-term demand trends, while the renewable-energy segment is mid-way through building two hydropower plants that are currently dragging on margins but are expected to become the company's highest-margin, most stable revenue source once they're finished. Our valuation work suggests the market is pricing the stock as if none of that upside exists.

What it is

ISDN reports five business segments. The three that matter most to the story:

Motion Control

Core motion-control components and engineering services (servo motors, drives, stages) that power industrial automation. The largest segment at 64.7% of revenue, but also the lowest-margin, at roughly 7% EBITDA margin.

Other Specialised Engineering

System-integration and precision-manufacturing work, including hinge/lock products and metal-injection molding. The highest-margin core segment at roughly 11% EBITDA margin, and 20.1% of revenue.

Renewable Energy

Mini-hydropower plants in Indonesia, both operating and under construction. Operating plants alone earn a 91.5% gross margin, but two plants still being built are diluting the segment's overall margin to around 7% for now. 13.4% of revenue today.

Valuation

Our base-case discounted cash flow estimate puts fair value at S$1.07 per share, about 56% above the current price; a more conservative cross-check method still implies 28% upside. The stock also trades at a roughly 68% discount to peer companies on EV/EBITDA (8.5x vs. a 26.9x peer median) and a 42% discount on forward P/E (17.1x vs. 29.4x), with a free-cash-flow yield of 4.5% that sits above the risk-free rate.

Why it's cheap — and why that should change

  • The market appears to be extrapolating last year's inflated headline growth rate without separating out the one-time construction-revenue boost from the renewable-energy project.
  • Two more hydropower plants are on track to finish in 2026, which should shift renewable energy from a margin drag into the group's highest-margin, most reliable earnings stream.
  • The balance sheet is only lightly indebted (0.4x net debt/EBITDA) and is expected to turn net-cash by 2028, even after funding the current construction spending — no financial overhang weighing on the stock.

Main risks

  • More than 60% of revenue comes from China, and demand is sensitive to the semiconductor capital-spending cycle — revenue fell 7.8% in 2023 during a downturn.
  • The two hydropower plants could be delayed past their guided 2026 completion date, pushing out the segment's margin turnaround.
  • The deep discount to peers could reflect a real difference in business quality — ISDN is a distributor/integrator rather than a component maker, and its dual listing may be less liquid — rather than a mispricing that closes.

Bottom line

We rate ISDN Holdings Attractive: our valuation work and its discount to peers both point to meaningful upside from today's price. But the share-price chart shows an uptrend that hasn't yet been confirmed by momentum, so the better approach is to build a position gradually or wait for a clearer signal, rather than buying all at once at the current price.
