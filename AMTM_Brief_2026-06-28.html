Amentum is one of the largest engineering- and technical-services contractors to the U.S. government — nuclear and environmental cleanup, space operations, intelligence and cyber. The work is steady, sold on multi-year government contracts. But the stock (~$20.25) trades well below our estimate of intrinsic value (~$35). The market is discounting three things that are already improving: debt from a 2024 merger, thin profit margins, and reported earnings that understate the cash the business actually generates.

What it is

Amentum does engineering and technical work for the U.S. government, across two segments:

Global Engineering Solutions

Nuclear and environmental cleanup, platform engineering and sustainment. The larger, more international book — steadier and lower-growth. FY25 margin 7.5%.

Digital Solutions

Intelligence, cyber, space and digital modernization. Mostly U.S., and roughly tripled by the 2024 Jacobs Critical Mission Solutions (CMS) deal. Faster-growing. FY25 margin 7.9%.

Valuation

At ~$20.25 the stock trades below both its peers and our estimate of intrinsic value. A discounted-cash-flow (DCF) valuation puts the base case near $38 a share, with a $26–49 range across scenarios (central ~$35). On multiples it's cheaper than comparable government contractors — 7.6x EBITDA vs ~9.4x for peers — and offers a higher free-cash-flow yield (8.9% vs 7.6%).

Why it's cheap — and why that should change

  • Debt is being paid down fast — about 3x EBITDA today, heading under 1x within a few years, after which cash can fund buybacks.
  • Margins are widening toward an 8.5–9% target as merger savings land — each point is worth roughly $3–4 a share.
  • A large non-cash accounting charge (~$479m) is masking reported profit; it fades on its own, lifting reported earnings.

Main risks

  • About 81% of revenue comes from one customer — the U.S. government — so budget fights or shutdowns can delay work.
  • Margins are the thinnest among peers, and the improvement is not yet proven.
  • Meaningful debt and goodwill from the merger leave little cushion if execution slips.

Bottom line

Attractive on value, but no rush. Intrinsic value (~$35) sits well above the ~$20.25 price, and the company is cheaper than its peers on every measure — yet the shares are still in a downtrend, about 20% below their falling 100-day average. Accumulate gradually into support near $19.80 and add once the trend repairs (a reclaim of ~$25). A small starter position is reasonable for patient investors; don't chase.
